Investment Philosophy

Structured discipline across market regimes

The strategy is built on the premise that durable risk-adjusted returns require explicit allocation rules combined with active options management — not passive hedging or discretionary flexibility. Each tier serves a distinct purpose; together they create a portfolio designed to generate alpha in both advancing and declining markets.

01
Regime Awareness

Market environments — trending bull, bear, sideways — demand different allocation responses. The framework adjusts options exposure and tier weights based on defined regime signals rather than reactive judgment.

02
Active Options Management

Protective puts are not held passively to expiration. Profits are taken on large down-move days; runners are managed with adjusted stops. After significant down legs, positions are reassessed before reloading — reentry requires confirmation of continued downside momentum. This active management is what separates the strategy's put P&L from a static overlay.

03
Tiered Risk Budgeting

Risk is allocated deliberately across tiers with explicit bounds. The core tier prioritizes capital preservation; the directional tier accepts higher variance for return enhancement; the speculative tier — including targeted long calls — is sized to be portfolio-immaterial at the position level.

04
Rules Over Discretion

Entry, exit, and rebalancing triggers are defined in advance. This removes the behavioral vulnerabilities — loss aversion, recency bias, overconfidence — that most systematically damage discretionary portfolio performance.

Portfolio Construction

Three-tier allocation framework

Academic grounding in core-satellite theory (Leibowitz & Bova) and risk-parity principles informs the tier boundaries. Each tier has defined allocation bounds, instrument scope, and rebalancing logic. An explicit cash reserve is maintained at all times as a strategic buffer, not a residual.

Risk Management

Explicit rules for drawdown control

Risk management in this framework is not reactive — it is pre-specified. The following mechanisms are active at all times regardless of market conditions or short-term performance.

01 — Hedge Trigger
Momentum-Based Put Deployment

Protective puts on the SPY/QQQ core position are purchased when a defined momentum signal crosses a threshold (e.g., 50-day MA crosses below 200-day MA). Tenor is 60–90 DTE; positions are actively managed and not held passively to expiration.

02 — Tier Bounds
Hard Allocation Ceilings

Each tier operates within defined allocation bounds. Tier III has a 4% hard ceiling; breaches trigger mandatory rebalancing. No single position in Tier II exceeds 7% of total portfolio.

03 — Cash Buffer
Residual Cash Reserve

Remaining portfolio weight after tier allocations is held in cash or equivalents. This provides optionality for hedge rolling costs, rebalancing, and opportunistic deployment in dislocated markets.

04 — Drawdown Limit
Portfolio-Level Stop

If total portfolio drawdown from peak exceeds 12%, Tier II positions are reduced to minimum allocation and Tier III is liquidated. Core hedge is reviewed for extension.

05 — Rebalance Cadence
Quarterly Structural Review

Tier allocations are formally reviewed quarterly against defined bands. Drift of more than 5 percentage points from target triggers rebalancing. Intra-quarter rebalancing occurs only on breach of hard limits.

06 — Regime Classification
Market Regime Tagging

Each month is classified as Bull, Bear, or Transitional based on price momentum, VIX level, and yield curve slope. Tier II allocation target adjusts ±5% based on regime tag. Tier I hedge budget adjusts ±2%.
